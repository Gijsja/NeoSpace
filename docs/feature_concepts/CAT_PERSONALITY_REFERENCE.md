# Cat Personality & Emotional States Reference

This list defines the 50 distinct states/traits for the CatSystem. These can be used as "Named Moods" calculated from the underlying PAD (Pleasure, Arousal, Dominance) values.

1. **Content** – Calm, balanced, and easygoing; prefers routine and stability.
2. **Playful** – Energetic, spontaneous, and easily excited by novelty.
3. **Curious** – Inquisitive and observant; driven to explore and investigate.
4. **Relaxed** – Laid-back and adaptable; unbothered by minor disruptions.
5. **Sleepy** – Low-energy and passive; prioritizes rest and comfort.
6. **Affectionate** – Social and bonding-oriented; seeks closeness and reassurance.
7. **Aloof** – Independent and self-contained; values personal space.
8. **Alert** – Vigilant and attentive; quick to notice changes in the environment.
9. **Anxious** – Cautious and sensitive; prone to overanalyzing stimuli.
10. **Irritated** – Short-tempered and reactive; low tolerance for stressors.
11. **Territorial** – Assertive and possessive; strongly defends boundaries.
12. **Fearful** – Timid and risk-averse; avoids unfamiliar situations.
13. **Confident** – Self-assured and bold; comfortable taking initiative.
14. **Mischievous** – Clever and rule-bending; enjoys pushing limits.
15. **Hungry** – Goal-driven and single-minded; focused on immediate needs.
16. **Protective** – Loyal and defensive; prioritizes safety of close bonds.
17. **Jealous** – Competitive and insecure; sensitive to perceived threats.
18. **Bored** – Understimulated and disengaged; seeks external input.
19. **Aggressive** – Dominant and confrontational; responds with force.
20. **Submissive** – Compliant and deferential; avoids conflict.
21. **Trusting** – Open and accepting; assumes positive intent.
22. **Startled** – Highly reactive; quick fight-or-flight responses.
23. **Lonely** – Socially dependent; craves interaction and validation.
24. **Demanding** – Assertive and vocal; expects needs to be met promptly.
25. **Focused** – Intentional and disciplined; able to ignore distractions.
26. **Overstimulated** – Sensitive and overwhelmed; low threshold for input.
27. **Defensive** – Guarded and skeptical; anticipates negative outcomes.
28. **Social** – Outgoing and interactive; energized by group settings.
29. **Grumpy** – Pessimistic and resistant; default negative affect.
30. **Satisfied** – Fulfilled and settled; low motivation for change.
31. **Watchful** – Patient and calculating; prefers to observe before acting.
32. **Skittish** – Nervous and unpredictable; easily spooked by change.
33. **Bold** – Adventurous and daring; seeks stimulation and challenge.
34. **Clingy** – Dependent and reassurance-seeking; dislikes separation.
35. **Stoic** – Emotionally restrained; steady under pressure.
36. **Restless** – Unable to settle; constantly seeking movement or activity.
37. **Proud** – Self-valuing and dignified; resists perceived disrespect.
38. **Opportunistic** – Flexible and strategic; exploits favorable moments.
39. **Lazy** – Energy-conserving and unmotivated; avoids unnecessary effort.
40. **Demure** – Quiet and reserved; avoids drawing attention.
41. **Hypervigilant** – Excessively alert; anticipates threats everywhere.
42. **Moody** – Emotionally variable; prone to rapid shifts in affect.
43. **Patient** – Tolerant and enduring; slow to react.
44. **Sneaky** – Cautious and indirect; prefers subtle approaches.
45. **Dominant** – Control-oriented and authoritative; expects compliance.
46. **Timid** – Shy and hesitant; reluctant to assert needs.
47. **Contentious** – Argumentative and challenging; resists control.
48. **Gentle** – Soft-mannered and non-confrontational; minimizes force.
49. **Curious-but-Cautious** – Exploratory yet restrained; balances risk and safety.
50. **Zen** – Deeply calm and centered; minimally reactive to the environment.
